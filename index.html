Liquidity Cycles and Tactical Positioning in a Post-ZIRP World

A process-first framework for institutional-grade macro decision making.

By Jespyucha • 8 min read • Macro / Risk / Execution

Macro signals matter only when converted into executable risk. The objective is not narrative elegance; the objective is to map liquidity state, quantify uncertainty, and deploy risk where asymmetry is favorable.

1. Policy impulse is your first filter

Begin with marginal change in policy stance and system liquidity. Markets often react to second derivatives, not levels. Repricing events tend to accelerate where consensus is anchored to stale macro assumptions.

2. Seek cross-asset confirmation

Validate your macro view through rates volatility, credit spreads, commodity breadth, and equity factor behavior. Durable regime shifts usually present coherence across multiple instruments before they appear in headlines.

3. Convert thesis into position architecture

Define invalidation, holding horizon, scenario weights, and drawdown tolerance before entry. A valid macro thesis can still fail through poor implementation discipline.
